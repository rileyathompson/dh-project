an hour before we land at newark as he was a sleep in the cabin cover warm he was sudden take extreme hoarse could hard breathe which surprise me great i awake him but twas all the same yet the child seem perfect well would play and smile when i got home put him to bed warm think it may be a sudden cold upon which he seem to breathe better and so continue til about eleven a m when a violent fever seize him and the hoarse increase i was much fright think it was the throat distemper and send for the doctor who was at a loss to conclude what the disorder was but order to the best of his skill but to no purpose eve the doctor said he was afraid the child would not live til morning and saturday eve told us plain that
