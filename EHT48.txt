the land very remarkable from the falls to this place which is about seventy mile the timber not very large at least upon the water side and i have no opportunity of see any other heavy rain all night we pass the green river about two o clock in the morning of the seventh about nine it begin to clear and our people went on shore to hunt and kill a tame cow which they mistook for a buffalo however it turn out to be very good beef they employ all the rest of the day jerkin it to preserve it which is done by cut it into small thin piece and run them on stick lay them on a scaffold underneath they make a small fire and a great smoke which in a few hour dry so as to keep a long time in the morning rain clear
