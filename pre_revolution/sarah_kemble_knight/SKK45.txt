rest after my journey my business lie unfinished by some concern at newyork depend thereupon my kinsman mr thomas trowbridge of newhaven must need take a journey there before it could be accomplish i resolve to go there in company with him and a man of the town which i engage to wait on me there accordingly december sixth we set out from newhaven and about eleven same morning come to stratford ferry which cross about two mile on the other side bait our horse and would have eat a morsel ourselves but the pumpkin and indian mix breed had such an aspect and the bare legged punch so awkward or rather awful a sound that we leave both and proceed forward and about seven at night come to fairfield where we meet with good entertainment and lodge and early next morning set forward to norowalk from its half
