the wabash a very fine river which run five hundred mile in to the country there are very fine french settlement upon this river one about one hundred eighty mile up call the opost i am inform the country is very healthy and the land very fertile and that it is a place of considerable trade about eight o clock we observe two canoe with indian make toward us we were prepare for their reception if their intention had been hostile the boat in company hail them and invite them to come onboard one of the canoe accept the invitation two indian a very near and handsome squaw with a young child they had former been of the delaware tribe but a number of them had leave that nation about fourteen year ago and went to live up the wabash they have been our very great enemy this war
